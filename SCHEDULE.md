# Schedule

*Data Structures & Algorithms in C++ — From Fundamentals to Interview-Ready*

Sixteen weeks, four lectures a week, 64 lectures total. Each lecture runs about 75 to 90 minutes live, and every recording stays in your library for life.

The cadence is fixed: **4 lectures per week**. Exact calendar dates are set per cohort and announced at enrollment, so this page tracks weeks and themes rather than specific days. Find your cohort's start date in the welcome email, then count forward from Week 1.

The ordering is deliberate. Weeks 1 and 2 cover complexity analysis and recursion before any data structure shows up. Those two are the lenses you'll use to read every topic that follows, so they come first. From there the course builds outward: linear structures, then pointer-based structures, then trees, then graphs, then the algorithmic paradigms (sorting, searching, backtracking, dynamic programming, greedy) that pull everything together.

Each week links to a detailed page under [`weeks/`](weeks/) with the per-lecture breakdown, the problem set, and the week's milestone.

## How to read the table

- **Week** links to the full breakdown for that week, including the problem set and how the lectures connect.
- **Lectures** are the four sessions for the week, numbered 1 through 64 across the whole course.
- **Milestone** is the bar you should clear by the end of the week. If you can't do what it describes, that's your signal to rework the week's problems before moving on.

Each block builds on the one before it. Recursion in Week 2 is what makes tree traversal in Week 8 feel routine. The backtracking template in Week 14 is the exhaustive search you then memoize into DP in Week 15. Skipping ahead tends to cost more time than it saves.

For the full problem bank, the pattern reference, and the projects, see the [main README](README.md).
