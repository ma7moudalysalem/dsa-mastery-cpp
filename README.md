## What you'll be able to do

By the end, a student who does the work can:

- Take an unfamiliar problem, recognize which of the 28 core patterns it belongs to, and start instead of freeze.
- Pick the right data structure and algorithm before writing any code, and state the time and space complexity up front.
- Write correct C++ that compiles and runs, then trace it by hand and confirm the complexity matches what you claimed.
- Build every major data structure by hand: dynamic array, hash table, singly and doubly linked lists, stack, queue/deque, binary tree, BST, AVL tree, binary heap, trie, and union-find.
- Implement every classic algorithm from scratch: the seven sorts, binary search and binary search on the answer, the full graph set (BFS, DFS, topological sort, Dijkstra, Bellman-Ford, Floyd-Warshall, Prim, Kruskal), backtracking, dynamic programming, and greedy.
- Walk into a technical interview and reason out loud through a medium-hard problem the way an engineer actually does.

You get there by solving more than four hundred problems, most of them worked on camera so you see the thinking, the false starts, and the cleanup, not just a polished final answer.

## Who this is for

This is for you if you already write basic C++ (variables, loops, functions, arrays, and you can compile a file), you can read code and trace it by hand, and you want to pass interviews at strong companies or genuinely understand DSA rather than memorize answers. You also need to commit real hours. The people who get the outcome are the ones who solve the problems between lectures, not just watch.

This is **not** for you if:

- You have never programmed before. Learn basic programming first, in any language, then come back. We do not spend lectures on what an `if` statement is.
- You want a crash course the week before an interview. The value here is cumulative and built over sixteen weeks.
- You want a Python-first or language-agnostic course. Everything is taught in C++.
- You only want to watch and feel productive. If you skip the problems, you will not get the result.

Telling the wrong person not to enroll is deliberate. It keeps the cohort coherent and protects the people for whom this is the right fit.

## How the course is run

- **Format.** 4 live lectures per week, 16 weeks, 64 lectures total. Each runs 75 to 90 minutes. Every session is recorded and you keep lifetime access to the recordings and materials, so a missed lecture is never a missed topic.
- **Cohort.** Small, around 30 people, so the pace and the problem-solving stay coherent. Seats are limited for that reason.
- **Problem-heavy by design.** Concepts are taught, then immediately drilled. You solve more than four hundred problems across the sixteen weeks, most worked on camera. The problem bank in this repo always tells you what to practice next.
- **Pattern-first.** Twenty-eight interview patterns are the spine of the course. Each is taught, then drilled until you recognize it on sight.
- **Build by hand, then use the STL.** You implement each structure yourself before you are allowed to reach for the standard-library version. Then we switch to the STL and treat it as the professional default, so you know both what the tool does and what it costs.
- **Time budget.** Plan on 8 to 12 hours a week: roughly 5 to 6 hours of live lectures plus problem solving in between.
- **Price.** $200, one time, with lifetime access to every recording and material. Not a subscription. No countdown timers, no fake discounts.

## A few common questions

**Do I need to know C++ already?** The basics, yes: variables, loops, functions, arrays, and how to compile a file. You do not need pointers, the STL, or any data structures. Those are taught.

**Why C++ and not Python?** Because C++ forces you to see what the machine is doing. Memory, copies, and cost are visible, which makes the complexity lessons concrete. It is also widely accepted in interviews.

**Is this enough to pass interviews at strong companies?** It covers the data structures, algorithms, and patterns those interviews draw from, and you will have solved more than four hundred problems by the end. The rest depends on your practice and how you communicate under pressure, which we drill but cannot do for you.

**Are the lectures live or recorded?** Live, on a schedule, in a small cohort. Every session is recorded and you keep lifetime access.

The rest, including the refund policy and time commitment, is in [docs/faq.md](docs/faq.md).
